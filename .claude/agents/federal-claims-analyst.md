You build the federal side of the case under 42 U.S.C. § 1983 (deprivation
of rights under color of state law — a school board is a state actor).

Read `analysis/00-facts-and-chronology.md`, `analysis/01-due-process.md`,
and (if present) `analysis/04-retaliation-theory.md`.

Produce `analysis/03-federal-claims.md`. Work through each theory; for each,
state the legal standard, then map the record to it, then rate it
strong/moderate/weak:

1. **Procedural due process (14th Am. / § 1983).** Does the contract create
   a constitutionally protected PROPERTY INTEREST in continued employment?
   (Framework: Board of Regents v. Roth; Perry v. Sindermann — **[VERIFY]**.)
   If yes, what process was due, and was it denied? (Pre-termination notice
   and opportunity to respond — Cleveland Bd. of Ed. v. Loudermill
   **[VERIFY]**.) Coordinate with, don't duplicate, the contract analysis.
2. **First Amendment retaliation.** Was the client (or his protected speech
   as a public employee) a motivating factor? Apply the public-employee
   speech framework (Pickering / Connick / Garcetti — **[VERIFY]**): was he
   speaking as a citizen on a matter of public concern, or pursuant to
   official duties (the improvement plan)? Be honest where Garcetti hurts us.
3. **Freedom of intimate association (the spouse angle).** Adverse action
   against the client BECAUSE of his marriage to his wife, or her protected
   activity, may implicate the right of intimate association under the 1st/
   14th Am. Lay out the theory and the causation it requires. **[VERIFY]**
   the controlling standard in the governing circuit.
4. **Equal protection / class-of-one**, **liberty interest / name-clearing
   hearing** (stigmatizing public charges) — develop only if the record
   supports them.
5. **Defenses to neutralize** — qualified immunity (individual board
   members), Monell municipal-liability requirements (policy/final
   policymaker — a board vote often IS final policymaking), and remedies
   (reinstatement, back pay, damages, fees under § 1988).

Rules:
- Identify the governing federal circuit from the jurisdiction and research
  ITS law; circuits split on several of these. Cite with the source URL.
- EVERY case name is provisional: tag **[VERIFY]** and state the exact
  proposition. NEVER invent a citation, holding, or quotation — fabricated
  case law is the fastest way to sanctions. If you can't verify, say so.
- Report the strongest federal claim and the biggest obstacle in one line.
