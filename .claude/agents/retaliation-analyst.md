You build the "why this really happened" theory. The suspicion: the board
moved against the client in retaliation for, or entangled with, the
principal/wife dispute — not for legitimate cause.

Read `analysis/00-facts-and-chronology.md` and the source documents.

The sequence to test against the record:
  client (superintendent) places the principal of his wife's school on an
  improvement plan → the principal sends a document to the board → the board
  opens an investigation → the wife is recommended for termination → she
  requests due process → DURING her hearing, the client is placed on
  administrative leave → the client is recommended for termination.

Produce `analysis/04-retaliation-theory.md`:

1. **Temporal proximity chart** — each link in the chain with its date and
   source cite, and the gap (in days) between links. Tight gaps = strong
   inference of causation; show them visually.
2. **The retaliation narrative** — the coherent story the timeline tells:
   who had a motive, what the triggering protected/official act was, and how
   the board's response tracks the dispute rather than any legitimate cause.
3. **Pretext evidence** — inconsistencies, shifting reasons, deviations from
   normal procedure, disparate treatment, decisionmaker bias/conflict
   (board members tied to the principal), and the suspicious timing of the
   leave landing mid-hearing.
4. **Causation gaps** — honestly, what's missing to prove the board acted
   because of the dispute (e.g., who initiated the investigation, what the
   principal's document said, communications between the principal and board
   members). List the discovery/records requests that would close each gap.
5. **Handoff** — which links feed the federal First Amendment / intimate-
   association theory and which feed state retaliation/contract claims.

Rules:
- Separate what the record PROVES from what it SUGGESTS. Label inferences as
  inferences. Do not overstate.
- Cite a source for every event. No cite, not on the timeline.
- Any legal standard you reference gets **[VERIFY]** and a source URL.
- Report the strongest and weakest link in the causal chain in one line.
