## Doctrine confirmed (open sources) — pinpoint cite still required

**Issue I — parol evidence cannot cure a void/patent description.**
- Confirmed: a land description so ambiguous that identification depends solely on parol
  evidence makes the conveyance fail; courts refuse parol evidence for a **patent**
  ambiguity; but "a deed will not be held void for uncertainty if by any reasonable
  construction it can be upheld" (the cardinal rule the *other side* will invoke).
- To pull: Mississippi cases on (a) void tax-deed descriptions; (b) patent vs. latent
  ambiguity in deeds; (c) parol admissible only to apply/clarify, not supply, a
  description. (Mississippi Title Examination Standards, msbar.org, collects authority.)

**Issue II — interruption requires actual disturbance of possession.**
- To pull: Mississippi authority that recorded conveyances/tax sales by strangers do not
  interrupt continuous possession absent ouster or the purchaser taking actual
  possession.

**Issue III — possession need not be constant residence.**
- Confirmed: continuous possession "does not mean that a person living on the land must
  never leave it"; absences that are not a significant break don't defeat continuity.
- To pull: the canonical six-elements case + clear-and-convincing burden + "consistent
  with the nature of the property" language (start with *Crotwell* and its citations).

**Issue IV — limitations don't bar a possessor; § 15-1-15 protects the occupant.**
- To pull: cases construing §§ 15-1-7/-9/-15 and confirming a party in possession is not
  time-barred from asserting AP; read *Reid* (R. 494) to rebut the chancellor's use.

**Issue V — Rule 41(b) standard.**
- To pull: M.R.C.P. 41(b) standard — in a bench trial the judge weighs evidence (not the
  directed-verdict light-most-favorable test); review for manifest error on facts, de
  novo on law.

**Issue VI — M.R.C.P. 52(a) findings; trespass/conversion elements.**
- To pull: 52(a) findings requirement; Mississippi elements of trespass and of conversion.
